Supersonic Combustion & Regenerative Microchannels

This simulation integrates the coupling between the internal gas path and a cryogenic hydrogen cooling jacket. Combustion gases transfer heat to the walls, which is carried away by high-pressure supercritical hydrogen.

  • Eckert Reference Temperature: Gas convective boundary layers are evaluated using the recovery temperature margin and reference enthalpy: T_ref = T_static + 0.5(T_wall - T_static) + 0.22(T_rec - T_static).
  • Sieder-Tate Correlation: Evaluates turbulent microchannel flow heat transfer coefficients with temperature-dependent hydrogen density and specific heat curves.
  • Applications: Hypersonic cruise vehicles, space access systems, and advanced combustion cooling.

Oblique Shock Wave Deflection Sizing

Supersonic flow passing over a wedge creates an oblique compression wave. This simulation shows the relationship between wedge deflection angle θ, upstream Mach M₁, and shock angle β.

  • θ-β-M Relation: Real-time bisection solver checks compressible equations. If the wedge deflection angle is too high, the shock detach-limit is crossed, and a detached bow shock wave is generated.
  • Fluid Deflection: Wave tracers demonstrate deceleration and deflection as they cross the shock boundary.
  • Applications: Supersonic inlets, aerodynamic shock tuning, and compressible CFD validation.

F1 Carbon-Carbon Brake Disc Thermal Transient

Carbon-carbon brake discs convert kinetic energy to heat. They must operate in a strict temperature window (300°C to 750°C) to prevent glazing (below 300°C) and rapid oxidation wear (above 750°C).

  • Lumped Capacitance Solver: Integrates thermal transients where heat input from braking kinetic energy competes with jet-impingement cooling and radiative emission.
  • Arrhenius Oxidation Rate: Quantifies the mass loss rate of carbon structure due to reaction with atmospheric oxygen at elevated temperatures.
  • Applications: High-performance motorsport braking systems and aerospace brake rotor sizing.

Tokamak Fusion Divertor Monoblock Exhaust

Nuclear fusion divertor assemblies must withstand steady heat fluxes exceeding 10 MW/m². Liquid water flows inside CuCrZr copper tubes shielded by tungsten tiles.

  • Boundary layer boiling: Subcooled water cooling is modeled, showing bubble nucleation enhancement.
  • Departure from Nucleate Boiling (DNB): If heat flux exceeds the critical limit (CHF), water vapor blankets the pipe interior, causing a drastic drop in heat transfer coefficient and wall meltdown.
  • Applications: Tokamak fusion reactor exhaust tiles and high-heat-flux power systems.

2D Finite Element Method (FEM) Truss Solver

Structural frame analyzer assembling the global stiffness matrix from element properties. Computes displacement vector, axial strains, and internal stresses under mechanical loads.

  • Stiffness Matrix Assembly: Solves K_global * U = F. Pinned supports fix boundaries. Member forces are color-mapped: Red for tension, Blue for compression.
  • Euler Buckling Limits: Compressive elements are validated against critical load capacity limits: P_crit = pi^2 * E * I / L^2 to check buckling collapse margins.
  • Applications: Aerospace structural frameworks, lightweight space trusses, and civil engineering.

NACA Airfoil Hess-Smith Panel Method Solver

Simulates 2D potential flow over NACA 4-digit airfoils. Renders stream velocity vectors, pressure coefficient profiles, and integrated lift curves.

  • Hess-Smith Method: Distributes source panels and uniform vortex singularities across the airfoil. Solves influence coefficient matrix satisfying zero normal flow at control points.
  • Kutta Condition: Enforces equal tangential velocity at trailing-edge panels to calculate circulation Gamma and aerodynamic lift coefficient Cl.
  • Applications: Wing profile sizing, wind turbine blade design, and aerodynamic lift sweeps.
